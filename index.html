How It Works

YOUR PATH TO AN ACCESSIBLE HOME

The VA SAH grant process can feel overwhelming. We're here to guide you through each step, from initial eligibility to handing you the keys.

1. DETERMINE ELIGIBILITY

The VA SAH grant is available to veterans with qualifying service-connected disabilities.

  • Loss or loss of use of both legs
  • Loss or loss of use of both arms
  • Blindness plus loss of use of a leg
  • Severe burns
  • ALS (any stage)
1

2. APPLY FOR YOUR GRANT

Submit VA Form 26-4555 to apply. The VA will review and issue your Certificate of Eligibility (COE).

  • Complete VA Form 26-4555
  • Provide medical documentation
  • Receive your COE
  • Current grant max: ~$109,986
2

3. FREE CONSULTATION

Meet with Built by Denny to discuss your needs, accessibility requirements, and vision for your home.

  • Review your grant status
  • Discuss accessibility needs
  • Explore design options
  • Get questions answered
3

4. DESIGN YOUR HOME

We create a custom floor plan that maximizes independence while meeting all VA requirements.

  • Custom accessible floor plan
  • Wheelchair-friendly layouts
  • Adaptive features throughout
  • VA compliance review
4

5. VA APPROVAL & PERMITS

We submit plans to the VA for approval and handle all local permits and inspections.

  • Submit plans to VA
  • Obtain building permits
  • Schedule inspections
  • Finalize contracts
5

6. BUILD & MOVE IN

Construction begins! We keep you informed and conduct a final walkthrough before handing over keys.

  • Quality construction
  • Regular progress updates
  • Final VA inspection
  • Move into your new home
6
