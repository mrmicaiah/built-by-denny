WHAT IS A ROLL-IN SHOWER?

A roll-in shower is a barrier-free shower designed specifically for wheelchair users. Unlike standard showers with curbs or lips, roll-in showers have a zero-threshold entry that allows wheelchairs to roll directly into the shower area without any obstacles.

For disabled veterans and anyone using a wheelchair, a properly designed roll-in shower means the difference between needing daily assistance and being able to bathe independently. It's one of the most important accessibility modifications for quality of life.

ROLL-IN SHOWER ADA DIMENSIONS

ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) guidelines specify minimum dimensions for roll-in showers to ensure they're truly accessible for wheelchair users. Here are the requirements we follow:

Standard Roll-In Shower: 30" x 60" minimum The most common size, allows full wheelchair entry and maneuvering
Alternate Roll-In Shower: 36" x 60" minimum Provides extra width for larger wheelchairs and easier transfers
Threshold Height: 1/2" maximum True roll-in showers should have zero threshold when possible
Fold-Down Seat: 17"-19" height Positioned for easy transfer from wheelchair
Grab Bars: Required on multiple walls Strategically placed for safety and support during transfers
Clear Floor Space: 30" x 48" outside shower Room for wheelchair positioning before entry

We design every roll-in shower to exceed ADA minimums when possible. More space means more independence and easier caregiving when needed.

ROLL-IN SHOWER FEATURES WE INSTALL

A great roll-in shower is more than just a flat entry. We install complete accessible shower systems with all the features needed for safe, independent bathing:

  • Zero-threshold entry (completely flush with floor)
  • Linear drain systems for proper water flow
  • Non-slip tile flooring with proper slope
  • Fold-down shower bench at transfer height
  • Padded, wall-mounted shower seats
  • Strategically placed grab bars
  • Handheld showerhead on adjustable slide bar
  • Thermostatic mixing valve (prevents scalding)
  • Single-lever or touchless faucet controls
  • Recessed soap dishes and storage at reachable heights
  • Shower curtain or glass panel options
  • Waterproof LED lighting

ROLL-IN SHOWER FAQs

How much does a roll-in shower cost?

Roll-in shower installation typically ranges from $5,000 to $15,000 depending on size, features, and your existing bathroom configuration. Complete accessible bathroom remodels with roll-in showers range from $15,000 to $35,000. For veterans with VA SAH Grants, much or all of this may be covered.

Can my VA SAH Grant pay for a roll-in shower?

Yes! Roll-in showers and accessible bathroom modifications are among the most common uses of VA SAH Grants. The grant provides up to $126,526 (2026 amount) for home accessibility modifications. We handle all VA paperwork and coordination.

How long does installation take?

A typical roll-in shower installation takes 5-10 business days depending on complexity. Complete bathroom remodels take 2-4 weeks. We'll provide a detailed timeline during your consultation.

Will water leak into the rest of my bathroom?

Not when properly installed. We use linear drain systems and carefully slope the floor so water flows toward the drain, not into your bathroom. Our waterproofing membrane system provides additional protection.

Can you install a roll-in shower in a small bathroom?

Often, yes. The minimum ADA size is 30" x 60", which can fit in many bathroom layouts. We may recommend removing a tub or reconfiguring the space. A home assessment will determine what's possible in your specific bathroom.

Do I need to modify my entire bathroom?

Not necessarily. We can install just the roll-in shower if your bathroom already has adequate door width and maneuvering space. However, most clients find it cost-effective to update the entire bathroom for full accessibility while we're already working.
