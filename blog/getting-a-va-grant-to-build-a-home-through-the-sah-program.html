GETTING A VA GRANT TO BUILD A HOME THROUGH THE SAH PROGRAM

January 8, 2026
By Denny Liuzzo

VA Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) — Overview
The VA Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) program assists eligible Veterans and Servicemembers with specific service-connected disabilities by providing a grant to construct, purchase, or modify a home to meet their accessibility needs, such as wheelchair access, safer bathing facilities, wider doorways, ramps, and more.

Grant Utilization The grant can be utilized for the following purposes:

  • Construction of a specially adapted home
  • Purchase of a home and subsequent modifications
  • Modification of an existing home that the applicant currently resides in
  • In certain instances, assistance in reducing the loan balance on an adapted home
Eligibility Criteria (High-Level Overview) Eligibility is contingent upon the presence of a qualifying service-connected disability. The VA has established specific criteria, including severe mobility impairments, loss of limb use, certain severe burns, and specific respiratory conditions. A VA representative will verify eligibility. Application Process (Steps)
  • Eligibility Verification and Documentation:
- Review VA disability rating/award information, medical documentation if necessary, and housing plans (build, buy, modify).
  • Application Submission:
- Submit an application online through VA.gov (search for “Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) grant”) or seek assistance from a VA-accredited representative or Volunteer Service Organization (VSO) (DAV, VFW, American Legion, county VSO).
  • Collaboration with a VA SAH Agent:
- Upon application submission, VA assigns a SAH agent who guides the process, reviews plans, and confirms requirements.
  • Decision on Housing Path:
- Obtain estimates, plans, and contractor/builder information.
  • VA Review and Approval
The VA reviews the scope and plans of the project and approves the grant usage. Funds are then disbursed in stages as the project progresses.

How a Builder “Specialist” Can Assist A builder familiar with the South Alabama Historical Society (SAH) can provide valuable assistance by:

  • Understanding common accessibility specifications (clearances, entryways, bathrooms, thresholds)
  • Coordinating with your SAH agent, lender, and inspectors
  • Maintaining organized documentation, bids, and change orders to ensure timely approvals
Quick Questions for Tailoring the Information 1) Are you inquiring as a Veteran or homeowner, or as a builder assisting clients? 2) Are you located in Huntsville, Alabama, or North Alabama? 3) Is the objective to construct a new home, purchase and modify an existing one, or modify an existing home?
